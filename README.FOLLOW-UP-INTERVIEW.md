# FOLLOW-UP INTERVIEW PREPARATION

> ⚠️ **IMPORTANT**: This document is for candidates who have progressed to the follow-up interview stage. If you're completing the technical assessment take home test, please refer to the main [README.md](./README.md) file instead.

---

Congratulations! If we have instructed you to read this file, this means you have passed the technical assessment and have been invited to the next stage, a follow-up technical interview.

Please read the following instructions carefully to help you best prepare for the interview and make the most of your time with the E.ON Next developers.

## What to Prepare

1. **Refamiliarize yourself with the code** and the choices you made in the coding exercise
2. **Review your commit history** and be ready to explain your development process and decision-making at each commit
3. **Review your testing approach and strategy** - be prepared to discuss why you chose specific testing methods and what trade-offs you considered
4. **If you used AI coding assistants** (such as GitHub Copilot, Claude Code, Cursor, etc.), be prepared to discuss your approach and how you integrated them into your workflow
5. **Ensure you can run your application locally** in dev mode and verify all associated processes work:
   - Development server runs without errors
   - Tests pass successfully
   - Type checking passes (`npx tsc --noEmit`)
   - Production build completes successfully
6. **Be prepared to run your application, tests, and screen share** your code editor with the developers during the interview

## What to Expect

1. **We will ask you questions about the approach and technical choices** you made when developing the application, including your testing strategy
2. **We will ask you questions about the different ways you could extend the application** in the future and the different approaches you might take and why
3. **We may ask you to further extend your application with a small feature during the interview** (potentially including adding tests). This is to assess your thought process and approach to work towards a solution rather than a perfect implementation

Good luck, and we look forward to discussing your work with you!
